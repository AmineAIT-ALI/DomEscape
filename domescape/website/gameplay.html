Gameplay Experience

The room is
the controller.

A player doesn't tap a screen. They open a real door. They cross a real sensor. They press a real button. The system reacts — physically, immediately, immersively.

The Scenario

DomEscape Lab 01
Reactivate the System

You enter a sealed laboratory. The automated control system has gone offline. Four sequential access codes must be unlocked to restore power and escape.

Each code is a physical interaction — a button pressed, a door opened, a sensor crossed, a remote keyed. The room is watching. It will respond.

The LCD display will guide you. The lamp will signal your progress. The plug will unlock your final clue. You have 60 minutes.

Four puzzles. Four physical interactions.

Why This Is Different

The game isn't on the screen.

Traditional interactive systems are click-based. DomEscape forces physical exploration. The interface is the room itself.

🌐
Physical World is the Interface
Players interact with real objects — real doors, real buttons, real movement. There is no touchscreen to tap through. The room is the UX.
⚡
Sub-second Feedback
From sensor event to physical response (lamp, LCD, plug) in under one second. The latency of the Z-Wave → Domoticz → PHP → Actuator chain stays imperceptible.
♾️
Infinitely Replayable
Scenarios are database rows. A different game can be configured and running within minutes, without changing hardware or code. Reset, reconfigure, replay.
